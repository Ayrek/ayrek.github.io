Weather Page Plan


Purpose Statement

To provide top professional accuracy and weather data to all the families across the world. Through this site millions of them can depend on accurate weather and detailed reports. In doing so, they will develop a strong trust and dependency on this company. This will be easily accomplished through the site's smooth navigation, simple interface, and straightforward weather reports. And all of the families will come to love and approve this exceptional site.

Audience

There about 3 billion of people are estimated to have an internet connection. And amongst those 3 billion are a lot of families. Lots of fathers, mothers, and children that want to be prepared for lays ahead of them. Parents most of all are expected to check out this website, so that they know how to best take care of their families. To allow the mother dress her children warm for the cold conditions, or a father to plan the next outside water activity, it's good to know the weather conditions. For the most part, everyone has a mobile device with an internet connection, and families generally have a tablet and desktop as well.

Persona

  • Fictional Name: Allan Walker
  • Job title: Sales Manager at Home Depot
  • Demographics: 36 years old, Married, father of five children, has bachelors in Business Management.
  • Goals and tasks: Allan is tasked to keep up customer satisfaction and the quality of his sales representatives. Although Allan has a lot on his plate, he is determined to keep strong in his job in order to provide for his family. He is mainly concerned with the welfare of his family and employees. He loves taking advantage of all weekends with his family that he can so that happiness and joy thrives in his family relationship.
  • Hobbies: Fishing, hunting, camping, hiking, and swimming.
  • Quote: "I am a father first then I am a manager."

Peer Review

Reviewer: Allan Alberro

Starting off with the smart phone device, I must say I really like it. The main reason I use weather apps are for quick glances of what the weather will be like, and I actually like that you have each day of the week visible versus having to scroll through or click next.

Moving onto the tablet, again, I really like the layout and the simplicity. The only thing I would maybe recommend would be having a search bar on the tablet. You have it on the browser and I'd like having that option open on a tablet. On the smart device, not so much because you're just looking for current/weekly weather.

The browser layout is great! It follows the F shape that is important for where the users eyes will be going. The only suggestion I could think of is having one main photo area at least for the top half. There does seem to be a number of images, which is fine, I've seen a lot of weather/news sites with a lot of boxes and images, but I think you could maybe have a main image on the browser and then other smaller ones.

Response

After consideration fo the comments and suggestions of Allen I have decided to add in a search bar in the IPad outline. However, I have decided to go ahead with my original plans of my desktop outline. I have decided to take this route because I believe that the user will easily enough identify the main element of the page. And I f increase the main image too much then it will be over dominating in the page.
